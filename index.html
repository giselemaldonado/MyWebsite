About Me

Hello, I’m Gisele Maldonado, a passionate and driven high school junior at the Energy Institute, with a deep love for space exploration and aerospace engineering. My journey has been fueled by a curiosity for the unknown and a commitment to learning, whether in the classroom, on the golf course, or exploring new instruments and languages.

I thrive in environments where I can advocate for others, especially women and minorities in STEM. This passion has not only helped me grow personally but has also sharpened my skills in coding, mechanics, research, communication, teamwork, and problem-solving. Every experience, from academic challenges to hands-on projects, has been an opportunity to build a stronger, more resilient version of myself.

As I look toward the future, my goal is to push the boundaries of aerospace engineering, particularly focusing on sustainability to protect both our planet and the cosmos. I am dedicated to opening doors for underrepresented groups in typically male-dominated fields, creating pathways where everyone can thrive. With higher education on the horizon, I’m eager to continue learning, sharing my knowledge, and contributing to a better world. Let’s connect and build something amazing together
